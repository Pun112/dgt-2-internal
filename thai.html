Tom Yum Goong (or Tom Yum Goong) is a classic Thai soup known for its hot and sour flavors. It features shrimp (goong), and it's a favorite for its fragrant broth and zesty taste. Hereâ€™s a traditional recipe to get you started

Tom Yum Goong Recipe

Ingredients: Broth: 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth 3-4 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger if unavailable) 2-3 Thai bird chilies, smashed (adjust to taste)

Soup: 200-300 grams (7-10 oz) shrimp, peeled and deveined 200 grams (7 oz) mushrooms (straw mushrooms or button mushrooms work well) 2-3 tomatoes, cut into wedges 1 small onion, thinly sliced 2-3 Thai bird chilies, sliced (adjust to taste) Seasoning: 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce (adjust to taste) 1-2 tablespoons lime juice (adjust to taste) 1 teaspoon sugar (optional) Garnish: Fresh cilantro, chopped Thai bird chilies, sliced (optional, for extra heat)

Instructions:

Marinate the Chicken: Prepare the Broth: In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and smashed bird chilies. Simmer for about 5 minutes to infuse the flavors into the broth. Add the Soup Ingredients: Add the mushrooms, tomatoes, and onions to the pot. Cook until the mushrooms are tender and the tomatoes have softened, about 5 minutes. Cook the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the pot and cook until they turn pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes. Season the Soup: Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar (if using). Adjust to taste, balancing the salty, sour, and spicy flavors. Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat and stir in the fresh cilantro. Add extra bird chilies if you like it spicier. Serve hot

Enjoy your Tom Yum Goong!
