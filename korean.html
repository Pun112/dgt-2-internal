Korean kimchi soup (kimchi jjigae) is a flavorful and hearty dish. Here's a basic recipe to get you started:

Kimchi Jjigae (Kimchi Soup) Recipe

Ingredients: 2 cups of well-fermented kimchi (cut into bite-sized pieces) 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil 1/2 pound of pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced (can substitute with tofu for a vegetarian version) 1 small onion, sliced 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced 1 tablespoon of ginger, minced (optional) 1-2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red chili paste) or gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes), adjust to taste 2 cups of beef or vegetable broth 1 tablespoon of soy sauce (or to taste) 1 teaspoon of sesame oil 1 block of tofu, cut into cubes (optional) 2 green onions, chopped 1 tablespoon of sugar (optional, to balance flavors) 1 cup of mushrooms (shiitake or button mushrooms), sliced (optional) 1 small zucchini, sliced (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare the Ingredients: If using pork, slice it thinly. Cut the kimchi into bite-sized pieces. Slice the onion and mushrooms, and cube the tofu if using. Cook the Pork: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sliced pork and cook until it's browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Add Aromatics: Add the sliced onion, garlic, and ginger (if using) to the pot. Saut√© until the onion is translucent and fragrant, about 3 minutes. Add Kimchi and Seasonings: Stir in the kimchi and gochujang (or gochugaru). Cook for about 2 minutes to let the kimchi flavor infuse into the pork. Add Broth and Simmer: Pour in the beef or vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. Add Vegetables and Tofu: If using tofu, mushrooms, or zucchini, add them to the pot. Simmer for another 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the flavors are well combined. Season to Taste: Stir in the soy sauce and sesame oil. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional soy sauce or sugar if needed. Finish and Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot, ideally with a bowl of steamed rice and some banchan (Korean side dishes).
